In this portfolio, I have put together some of the key projects that I have worked on in the last few years.

I decided to treat the process like I would any other UX problem: with research, early stage design, testing, and mockups. I built my site using HTML, CSS and Javascript. Because it highlights both my front-end development knowledge and my design process, I chose to include it as one of my portfolio‚Äôs case studies.

PORTFOLIO PROJECT

The Challenge

The goal of this project was to consolidate my best works in an easy to consume website. I also wanted to give myself a personal brand identity.

Research

I found a 2019 article on the top 21 UX resumes and their designers: people who have worked at Google, Facebook, Spotify etc.

Following through each portfolio I quickly realised there were two main formats being used. Individual cards in a grid format, or large, full-width scroll cards displaying their work.

Early Design Sketches

I sketched out what the two types of portfolios might look like. Some advice from a fellow designer ended up shaping my choice between them. He told me that people would judge my capability based on the least-impressive piece of my work.

I narrowed down my work to three case studies which best represented my skills. This fit best in the full-width scrolling card display, so I went with that.

Branding

Before I could start with the higher fidelity designs of my site, I needed to do some self branding. First I needed a logo. I did some rough sketches of variations of my initials ‚ÄòSH‚Äô and my name.

A round of testing with Graphic and UX Designers identified two logos as clear favourites: my full name in cursive, and the logo circled in green. After mocking up both I settled on the simple ‚ÄòS‚Äô as it took up less space and looked more professional overall.

A photo I took of a sunset in the Maldives made it easy to decide on my colours. The pallet is a good balance of cool professionalism in the darker navy, with orange and yellow tones providing some warmth.

My logo with different coloured tiles.

Higher Fidelity Designs

Now that I had my logo, color range and decided the format of my portfolio, I started on some higher fidelity designs. I went through a couple of different variations of colour using Sketch.

I tested the different coloured home page designs, and received overwhelming responses preferring the dark navy look. ‚ÄúIt‚Äôs more professional and you can always use pops of colour in your illustrations‚Äù was the main feedback.

I designed the layout of the home page, going through a lot of different combinations of tiles and layouts.

Development

I hosted my site on Github Pages which made everything easy. The task that consumed the most time was making everything responsive on all different devices. For the Sunbrella section in particular, the image itself changes depending on the size of your screen.

Final round of testing

I sent my finished portfolio to several recruiters to gather feedback. I received three main critiques, 1) that there needed to be a way to navigate to the next/previous project at the bottom of each case study, 2) the images on mobile needed to be larger, and 3) I needed to condense my words.

If you‚Äôve read this far, hopefully I succeeded with number 3!

What I would do differently next time

I am really happy with how my portfolio turned out.

Next time, I would probably spend longer considering what parts of each project are the most interesting, and seek to highlight these in my layout and graphics.

Feel free to give me feedback on your experience. I am a keen and fast learner and am always looking to improve my website.
